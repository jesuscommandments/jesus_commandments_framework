      We do not say that the Old Testament commandments are mandatory for each Christian. We can learn how the apostles thought about this subject in Acts chapter 15. Paul and Barnabas went back to Jerusalem to talk about the law of Moses, if it still applies for Gentiles. Now, James, one of the eldest, concludes this in verse 19 and 20:

      "Wherefore my sentence is, that we trouble not them, which from among the Gentiles are turned to God: But that we write unto them, that they abstain from pollutions of idols, and from fornication, and from things strangled, and from blood."


      James (and all the elders agreed) gave 4 examples of the law of Moses. Notice 3 of them were related to food, which is probably to make sure there are no obstacles for Jews and Gentiles to have communion together. But the main thing you have to keep in mind that James speaks this to just converted Gentiles. You don't need to make it too difficult for just converted Gentiles! Although, if you want to endure the storms you will face in the future, you need to get rooted in Christ!

      Jesus himself told in Matthew 5:17 "Think not that I am come to destroy the law, or the prophets: I am not come to destroy, but to fulfil". Jesus came to fulfil the law. Since Jesus gave Himself as sacrifice for our sins (past, present and future), all the commandments regarding the sacrifices are fulfilled. He also fulfilled the rest of the commandments and showing us the heart behind the law. But that doesn't mean we don't need to apply those commandments to our lives. Jesus said "Be ye therefore perfect, even as your Father which is in heaven is perfect." (Matt 5:48)

      We made this website, and overview of Jesus Commandments, to see how Jesus fulfilled many of the Old Testament laws, by showing the heart behind the laws. You can see all the related Bible verses and commandments we found. It are all precious gifts from God. Ready for us to unpack!


      Psalms 112:1 says "Praise ye the LORD. Blessed is the man that feareth the LORD, that delighteth greatly in His commandments"

      By the plain meaning of the verse "that feareth the LORD" refers to the negative commandments in our holy Torah (First 5 books of the Bible), while the phrase "delighteth greatly in His commandments" refers to the positive commandments in the Torah. The intent of is is that a person should watch himself and take care not to transgress the negative commandments and to carry out the positive commandments.

      Wherever the Torah says me'od ("very much" or "greatly"), it means to convey "without end or limit", something that will never terminate. The man "that delighteth greatly in His commandments" means one's infinite, boundless longing and yearning, after he ponders and considers the greatness of a mitzvah (a single commandment) itself, how far-reaching its effect is. Then even its reward is to be reckoned as nought and nothing compared with the great value of doing the mitzvah itself. So there is no end or limit to a person's longing to fulfill a mitzvah.


      In the Torah are 613 commandments (mitzvoth), according to Rambam (shortened name to Rabbi Moshe ben Maimon, of Maimonides. Lived ~1135-1204 AD). Most Jews agree upon this number of commandments.

      Many of the mitzvoth from the Torah cannot be followed (yet) at this time because the Temple is not there at the moment (think of the commandments about the Temple, sacrifices, and some for priests and Levites) . In addition, the commandments for which Israel needs a theocratic government are also not applicable at this time. Other commandments (especially agricultural ones) are only applicable in the land of Israel (that is why an Israelite can best keep the Mitzvot in the land of Israel today). According to Rabbi Yisrael Meir haKohen (who is called the Chafetz Chayim), 77 positive and 194 negative mitzvoth can currently be exported outside of Israel. In the list we use the CC coding. This coding refers to the layout of the Chafetz Chayim (From 'The Concise Book of Mitzvoth');

      • CCA means a positive mitzwe (which can now also be carried out outside of Israel).
      • CCN is the negative mitzwe (which can now also be carried out outside of Israel).
      • CCI means that this mitzvah can only be performed in the State of Israel.
